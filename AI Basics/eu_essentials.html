EU AI Act

What happened

EU created one rulebook for AI (in force since 2024).
Risk tiers: banned, high-risk, limited/minimal.
Goal: stop harmful uses and set safeguards for sensitive areas.

What’s happening now

Banned uses already illegal (e.g., social scoring; certain live biometric ID).
EU AI Office is active to oversee big model makers.
General-purpose model providers rolling out documentation, safety tests, copyright measures.

What’s next

High-risk uses (hiring, credit, education, essential services) must pass safety checks, log activity, and keep human oversight.
More visible labels (deepfake notices, "AI-assisted" tags) and clearer explanations.
Regulators phase in audits/enforcement through 2026-27.

Bottom line

Harmful uses are shut down, powerful models are supervised, and high-stakes AI gets formal safety and oversight.
